Quick Facts

South Korea at a glance

South Korea is a dynamic, technologically advanced country with a rich cultural heritage. From the neon-lit streets of Seoul to ancient palaces, Buddhist temples, and dramatic coastlines — it offers an extraordinary blend of old and new. Korean culture (K-pop, Korean cuisine, Korean cinema) has taken the world by storm and living here puts you right at the source.

Education is extremely highly valued in Korean society, creating enormous and consistent demand for qualified English teachers across public schools, private academies (hagwons), universities, and corporations.

Cost of Living

What does life in South Korea actually cost?

South Korea offers one of the best financial packages for English teachers globally. Most positions include free housing (or a housing allowance), and the combination of a competitive salary and low cost of living means teachers can save R$500–$1,000 or more per month.

Living in South Korea

What to expect when you arrive

🏠
Accommodation
Most teaching contracts in South Korea include free furnished accommodation — either in a school-provided apartment or via a housing allowance. This is one of Korea's biggest advantages for teachers. The accommodation is typically a small but functional studio apartment (officitel) close to your school.
🍜
Food & Culture
Korean food is extraordinary — kimchi, bibimbap, Korean BBQ, tteokbokki, and endless varieties of banchan (side dishes). Eating out is very affordable, with a full meal often costing ₩6,000–₩12,000. Convenience stores (GS25, CU, 7-Eleven) are everywhere and sell surprisingly good, cheap food around the clock.
🚇
Getting Around
Seoul's metro system is one of the best in the world — clean, punctual, affordable, and fully signposted in English. T-money cards work across metro, buses, and taxis. KTX bullet trains connect major cities quickly. In rural areas, intercity buses are reliable and comfortable.
🏥
Healthcare
South Korea's healthcare system is outstanding and very affordable. Employers are required to enrol teachers in National Health Insurance (NHI), splitting the cost with you. Hospitals and clinics are modern, well-equipped, and English-friendly in larger cities. Dental and cosmetic care is world-class and extremely affordable.
📱
Technology & Connectivity
South Korea has the fastest internet in the world. 5G is widespread, and getting a local SIM card is straightforward for foreign residents. KakaoTalk is the essential messaging app — everyone uses it. Naver Maps is more accurate than Google Maps for Korean navigation.
🤝
Social Life & Community
South Korea has a large, active expat teaching community particularly in Seoul, Busan, and Daegu. Meetup groups, language exchanges, hiking clubs, and social sports leagues make it easy to build a rich social life. Koreans are welcoming once connections are made, and learning basic Korean phrases goes a long way.
Visas & Entry

How to get your work visa for South Korea

South Korea's E-2 visa is specifically designed for native English-speaking teachers and is one of the most straightforward teaching visas in the world. Your employer handles most of the process once you have a job offer.

The EPIK (English Programme in Korea) government programme is an excellent, well-organised route for first-time teachers — it places you in public schools nationwide with strong support structures. CELTA-qualified candidates are strongly preferred and often receive higher starting salaries.

1 Apply to EPIK or secure hagwon/school job offer
2 Get documents apostilled (degree, background check)
3 Apply for E-2 visa at Korean consulate
4 Arrive, get medical check, register alien card (ARC)
E-2 Visa Requirements
  • ✓ Citizen of an approved English-speaking country
  • ✓ Bachelor's degree (any subject)
  • ✓ Apostilled criminal background check
  • ✓ Apostilled degree certificate
  • ✓ Signed contract from Korean employer
  • ✓ CELTA qualification (preferred, may increase pay)
Processing time: 2–6 weeks once documents are submitted. One of the faster teaching visa processes globally.
