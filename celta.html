About CELTA

The gold standard in English language teaching

CELTA (Certificate in English Language Teaching to Adults) is awarded by Cambridge Assessment English — part of the University of Cambridge — and is recognised by employers in over 100 countries worldwide.

Unlike many online qualifications, CELTA requires candidates to complete observed, assessed teaching practice with real adult learners. This makes it uniquely valuable to employers who know exactly what a CELTA-qualified teacher can do in the classroom.

At Orbit Teaching Academy, we offer multiple course formats to fit your schedule and lifestyle — whether you want to complete the course intensively in 3–4 weeks, or spread it across 10–15 weeks part-time.

The Curriculum

What you'll learn

The CELTA curriculum is designed and assessed by Cambridge Assessment English. Every element builds the practical skills you need to walk into any classroom in the world and teach effectively from day one.

🧠
Language Analysis & Awareness
Develop a deep understanding of English grammar, vocabulary, and phonology — so you can explain it clearly and confidently to adult learners at any level, from complete beginners to advanced speakers.
📝
Lesson Planning & Design
Learn to plan engaging, learner-centred lessons using proven frameworks. You'll design every stage with purpose — from warm-up activities to presentation, practice, and production.
🎤
Observed Teaching Practice
The defining feature of CELTA. You'll deliver real lessons to real adult learners, observed and assessed by Cambridge-certified tutors who provide detailed, constructive feedback after every session.
🔍
Learner Needs & Contexts
Understand how to assess and respond to learner needs, learning styles, and language backgrounds. You'll develop skills in error correction, elicitation, and differentiated instruction.
🗣️
Classroom Management
Build confidence managing diverse adult classrooms — from setting up pair and group work, to giving instructions clearly, to managing mixed-level classes and keeping learners engaged throughout.
📚
Materials & Resources
Learn to evaluate, adapt, and create teaching materials. You'll work with coursebooks, authentic texts, audio, video, and digital resources — and know how to choose the right tool for each lesson.
Application Process

How to apply

Our application process is designed to be straightforward and supportive. We want to understand your background and goals — and make sure CELTA is the right fit for you before you commit.

1
Complete the Online Application
Fill in our short online application form covering basic demographic information and your interests in teaching. It's quick and straightforward — designed to help us understand who you are and what you're looking for.
⏱ Approx. 3–5 minutes
2
Complete a Pre-Interview Writing Task
You'll receive a language awareness task to complete independently. This is designed to assess your general aptitude and interest in language — not your existing teaching knowledge. You're welcome to use online resources to help, but we encourage you to complete it in your own words rather than using AI tools. This task is a valuable part of your preparation for the course itself.
⏱ Approx. 45 minutes – 1.5 hours
3
Meet with a Course Advisor
One of our course advisors will review your pre-interview writing task and have a conversation with you about your interest in the CELTA and your goals for teaching abroad. This is a two-way conversation — it's also a chance for you to ask us anything. Together, we'll determine your suitability for the programme and find the right course format for your schedule.
💬 Personal conversation — no pressure

FAQ

Your CELTA questions, answered

Everything you need to know before you apply — honest answers, no fluff.

You must be 18 years or older and have a C1 or higher level of English — that's advanced level, meaning you can discuss complex topics clearly, accurately, and with nuance. A university degree is not required to enrol on the CELTA itself, but you will need a high school diploma or equivalent. Note that while CELTA doesn't require a degree, many international teaching employers and visa programmes do — our advisors will help you understand what's needed for your specific destination.

In a physical classroom alongside peers, feeding off the energy of the room and getting face-to-face feedback?
